A **correlated equilibrium** is a correlated strategy pair ($z_{ij} = \mathbb{P}(\mathcal{R}=i, \mathcal{C}=j)$) in a two-player game with payoff matrices $A$ and $B$ such that

$$
\mathbb{P}(\mathcal{R} = i) > 0 \implies \mathbb{E}\left( a_{i,\mathcal{C}} \mid \mathcal{R} = i\right) \geqslant \mathbb{E}[a_{\ell, \mathcal{C}} \mid \mathcal{R} = i], \qquad \forall i,\ell \in [n]
$$
