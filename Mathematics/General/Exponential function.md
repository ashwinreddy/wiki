The healthy way to think about the **exponential function** is by its [[Maclaurin series]],

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:defn}
\boxed{
\exp x \triangleq \sum_{k=0}^\infty \frac{x^k}{k!}
}
\end{equation}

as a solution to the differential equation

\begin{equation}
f' = f,\quad f(0) = 1
\end{equation}

Equation \ref{eq:defn} sensibly holds for [[real numbers|real number]], [[complex numbers|complex number]], and [[quaternions|quaternion]].

\begin{equation}
\exp x = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{x}{n}\right)^n
\end{equation}
