The **integral** is a [linear operator]() that gives the area under a curve, at least under the Riemann sum interpretation.

\begin{equation}
\int\limits_a^b{\left[f(x)\right]\,\mathrm{d}g(x)} = \int\limits_a^b{\left[f(x)g'(x)\right]\,\mathrm{d}x}
\end{equation}

### Properties

\begin{equation}
\int\limits_b^a{f(x)\,\mathrm{d}x} = -\int\limits_a^b{f(x)\,\mathrm{d}x}
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
\int\limits_a^b{f(x)\,\mathrm{d}x} = 0
\end{equation}
