Two [[observables|observable]] $A$ and $B$ are **compatible** if any of the following equivalent conditions:

a) If you measure $A$, then $B$, then $A$ you get the same measurement for $A$.
b) $A$ and $B$ commute. 

\begin{equation}
[A, B] = 0
\end{equation}

c) $A$ and $B$ have a common eigenbasis.

The [[uncertainty principle]]

\begin{equation}
\sigma_A^2 \sigma_B^2 \geqslant \left(\frac{1}{2\iota} \langle \left[A,B\right] \rangle\right)^2
\end{equation}
