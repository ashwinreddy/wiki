The **Hamiltonian operator** in quantum mechanics gives the total energy. 

\begin{equation}
\hat{H} = \hat{T} + \hat{V}
\end{equation}

When we use the canonical substitution $p \to -i\hbar(\partial / \partial x)$,

$$
\hat{T} = \frac{\hat{p}^2}{2m} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial ^2 }{\partial x^2}
$$

Since any general wave function is a linear combination of the stationary states, we could have

$$
\Psi(x,t) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n \Psi_n(x,t)
$$

$$
\sum_{n=1}^\infty |c_n|^2 = 1
$$

$$
\langle H \rangle = \sum |c_n|^2 E_n
$$
