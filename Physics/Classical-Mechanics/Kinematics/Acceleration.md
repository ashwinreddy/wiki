The acceleration is the [[derivative]] of [[velocity]].

\begin{equation}
\vec{a} = \dv{\vec{v}}{t}
\end{equation}

# Uniform Acceleration Motion

A handy special case is when the acceleration is a constant vector. 

\begin{equation}
\vec{r}(t) = \frac{t^2}{2}\vec{a} + t\vec{v}_0 + \vec{r}_0
\end{equation}
