The **Lorentz force** is the superposition of the electric and magnetic forces.

$$
\vec{F} = q\left(\vec{E}+\vec{v}\times\vec{B}\right)
$$

If we divide volume out of the equation, we get a similar equation

$$
\vec{\mathrm{f}} = \rho\vec{E}+\vec{\jmath}\times\vec{B}
$$

# Lagrangian

## Classical

$$
\mathcal{L} = \frac{m}{2}\dot{r}^2 + q\left(\vec{A}\cdot\vec{r}-V\right)
$$

## Relativistic

$$
=-mc^{2}{\sqrt {1-\left({\frac {\dot {\mathbf {r} }}{c}}\right)^{2}}}+q\mathbf {A} (\mathbf {r} )\cdot {\dot {\mathbf {r} }}-q\phi (\mathbf {r} )
$$
