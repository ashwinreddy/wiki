Maxwell's equations describe how to create electric field $\vec{E}$ and magnetic field $\vec{B}$ using an electric charge density $\rho$ and current vector $\vec{j}$.

\begin{gather}
\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{E} = \rho/\varepsilon_0 \\\\\
\vec\nabla \times \vec{B} = \mu\_0 \vec{j} + \mu\_0 \varepsilon_0 \pdv{\vec{E}}{t} \\\\\
\vec\nabla \times \vec{E} = - \pdv{\vec{B}}{t} \\\\\
\vec\nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0
\end{gather}
